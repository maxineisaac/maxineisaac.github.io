01Context

a gap i kept noticing.

Rooms for women in their twenties tended to feel like transactions — networking, not connection. grlsmeetwrld is my answer: an editorial event series and community brand built on the opposite promise — depth, not networking. I co-founded it and led it as creative director, from the first idea to a launch that sold out before a single ad ran.

The strategic bet was restraint: one volume at a time, each anchored to a single conversation worth having in person, in a voice that's sparse and lowercase by design — a small refusal to perform.

02My Role

creative director, end to end.

I owned the brand from positioning to production. That meant the strategy and manifesto, an i-D-inspired visual identity (the g/ wordmark, palette and one-word-per-post system), an original 30-card conversation deck designed to collapse small talk at the table, and the event itself — venue, vendors and the experience.

The full brand system — palette, voice, the launch feed — lives on the dedicated site. This page is the short version: what the work was, and what it did.
