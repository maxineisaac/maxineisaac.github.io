01Context

four quarters down, one weekend to answer it.

Starbucks Singapore was facing a fourth consecutive quarter of profit decline — driven by boycotts, high drink prices, and the rise of premium home coffee machines. The brief: design a multi-sensory experiential campaign that would deepen emotional connection with young professionals while reinforcing Starbucks' premium brand identity.

02Brand Analysis

a clearer diagnosis than the brief.

I led the company analysis. A SWOT exercise pointed to a sharper read than the brief had: the brand's strengths — customisation and the "third place" atmosphere — were being undercut by long wait times and overwhelming menus, and the community-coffeehouse identity wasn't being reinforced anywhere in the digital experience.

So the campaign had to do two things at once — fix a digital absence in the short term, and earn back a category position long term.

03Persona

everything tested against felicia.

I built Felicia Tay — a 25-year-old marketing executive, middle-to-upper income, living in the city. I covered her habits, values, preferences, needs and pain points, and tested every concept decision against her.

The persona shaped every downstream choice. The "exclusive mug for completing all stamps" reward came straight from a persona finding: this segment values tangible takeaways and exclusive merchandise more than discounts.

Felicia wouldn't post a discount. She would post a mug she had to earn.

04Concept

"bean there, met you" — five stations, one rally.

My group of five built Bean There, Met You — a two-day Valentine's weekend event (14–15 February 2025) at PLQ Plaza, structured around five interconnected stations and a stamp rally.

  • Specialty coffee tasting
  • Hands-on brewing
  • Latte art
  • DIY cup sleeves
  • Photobooth / photowall

Complete all four core stations and you earned an exclusive Starbucks mug — a deliberately scarce, deliberately shareable object designed to do the social-media work after the event was over.

05Social Rollout

nano-influencers over reach.

I designed the social collaterals — event promotion posts, captions, and the rollout plan for Instagram and TikTok. We used a nano-influencer engagement strategy — small audiences, high trust — to drive FOMO without burning the budget on reach we couldn't activate.

06Outcome

built to keep working after it ended.

A two-day event engineered to keep working once everyone went home — the earn-it mug doing the social work after the weekend closed.
